# ESGCheck — Master Company Document

## 1. Overview

ESGCheck is a practical ESG readiness platform for B2B SMEs and suppliers facing rising ESG information requests from customers, procurement teams, lenders, investors, and larger supply-chain partners.

Its purpose is to help companies understand where they currently stand on ESG and prepare for rising sustainability information requests by transforming the documents they already have into a clear ESG readiness overview. Instead of requiring a company to begin with a full consulting engagement or a heavy enterprise ESG system, ESGCheck starts with the materials many companies already possess: policies, internal records, supplier information, and company reports.

At its current stage, ESGCheck is designed to deliver a credible first ESG readiness overview: a structured output that maps available evidence, identifies important gaps, outlines practical next steps, and includes an indicative score with rationale. The goal is not to replace assurance, certification, or deep specialist advisory work. The goal is to reduce complexity, lower the barrier to action, and make ESG readiness work more practical for smaller companies.

ESGCheck is being built from Switzerland, with a strong emphasis on practicality, credibility, and privacy-conscious data handling.

## 2. Mission

ESGCheck helps B2B SMEs and suppliers prepare credible answers to ESG information requests by turning existing company documents into a clear overview of available evidence, important gaps, and practical next steps.

## 3. Long-Term Vision

ESGCheck aims to become a trusted ESG workflow platform for growing SMEs in Switzerland and, over time, across Europe.

It begins with practical first readiness overviews and is intended to evolve progressively into more robust, trusted, and professionally usable ESG workflows as the methodology matures, expert validation deepens, and market trust grows.

Over the long term, ESGCheck aims to support certification-oriented use cases and, where the necessary methodological, institutional, and regulatory conditions can be met, contribute to a path toward full ESG certification.

## 4. Why ESGCheck Exists

ESGCheck emerged from the observation that sustainability expectations have been rising for years while many SMEs still lack a practical way to understand where they stand.

For smaller companies, the pressure often arrives before formal reporting obligations do. It comes through customers, procurement teams, supply-chain requirements, banks, investors, and broader market expectations. Many companies are not yet being asked for a full reporting-grade output. They are being asked to demonstrate maturity, provide information, and show that they are taking ESG seriously.

Regulatory simplification does not remove ESG pressure for SMEs. In many cases, it changes the form of that pressure. Fewer SMEs may be directly subject to full sustainability reporting obligations, but many will still need to provide structured ESG information to larger customers, financial institutions, procurement teams, and supply-chain partners.

At the same time, traditional ESG work remains too expensive, too manual, too consultant-heavy, or too operationally complex for many SMEs.

ESGCheck is founded on the view that AI can be used in a practical, non-hyped way to make ESG readiness work significantly more accessible. Rather than replacing expertise, it can reduce manual effort, organize information more efficiently, surface missing data more clearly, and lower the cost of obtaining a first meaningful ESG readiness overview.

ESGCheck is also based on a broader conviction: if ESG becomes more visible, understandable, and actionable for smaller companies, more businesses may choose to invest in it seriously. That, in turn, can strengthen credibility, improve readiness for stakeholder questions, and make responsible business practice more tangible in day-to-day decision-making.

## 5. Why ESGCheck Matters Beyond Compliance

ESGCheck is not only about helping companies keep up with regulation.

It also matters because ESG increasingly affects how companies are perceived by customers, partners, investors, lenders, and larger organizations in their ecosystem. For many growing SMEs, ESG is becoming part of business credibility, market readiness, and long-term brand strength.

A company that can show it has taken ESG seriously is often in a stronger position to:

- respond to stakeholder expectations more confidently
- strengthen trust with customers and partners
- improve its brand image and business credibility
- demonstrate maturity to investors, lenders, procurement teams, and larger counterparties
- identify internal gaps before they become urgent or externally visible problems

ESGCheck is therefore intended to serve both as a practical business tool and, over time, as part of a broader shift in which ESG becomes more visible, more actionable, and more worthwhile for SMEs to engage with meaningfully.

## 6. What ESGCheck Is

ESGCheck is a practical ESG readiness platform.

It is not currently positioned as a certification product, a legal assurance mechanism, or a full substitute for formal sustainability reporting. At its current stage, it is designed to provide a credible first ESG readiness overview based on existing company documents.

The product is built around a document-first logic:

- companies upload policies, internal records, supplier or operational materials, and company reports
- ESGCheck analyzes those materials against structured ESG criteria
- the system generates a first structured readiness overview
- the output includes evidence mapping, gap identification, practical next steps, and an indicative score with rationale

This makes ESGCheck particularly suitable for SMEs that do not yet have a mature ESG workflow but already have relevant information scattered across existing documents.

## 8. Problem ESGCheck Solves

Many SMEs face a growing ESG problem from several directions at once:

- customers and larger counterparties increasingly request ESG-related information
- procurement and supply-chain expectations are rising
- investors, lenders, and financing stakeholders are paying more attention to ESG maturity
- markets and customers increasingly notice sustainability-related credibility
- regulation and voluntary reporting expectations are becoming more relevant over time

Yet traditional ESG work is still often too expensive, too manual, too consultant-heavy, or too complex for smaller organizations.

For many SMEs, the immediate problem is not producing a perfect final report.

Many are not trying to produce a full formal sustainability report at all. They are trying to answer practical ESG questions from customers, banks, investors, procurement teams, and larger counterparties. They need to understand what information they already have, what is missing, and how close they are to common voluntary SME reporting expectations such as VSME.

The first problem is more basic:

They do not yet know clearly where they stand, what they already have, what is missing, and what they should improve first.

ESGCheck is designed to solve exactly that first problem.

It turns existing company material into a first ESG readiness overview that helps a company understand:

- its current ESG readiness position
- what evidence is already available
- where the most important gaps are
- what should be improved next

This makes ESGCheck especially relevant for B2B SMEs and suppliers whose ESG pressure arrives through business relationships rather than direct regulation.

## 11. What the Product Does in Practice

At a high level, ESGCheck follows a simple workflow:

1. A company uploads relevant ESG-related documents.
2. The system ingests and analyzes those documents.
3. The content is assessed against structured ESG criteria.
4. ESGCheck generates a first structured ESG readiness overview.
5. The company receives a clear overview of its ESG readiness, available evidence, gaps, next steps, and indicative score.

In external communication, the emphasis should remain on customer value rather than technical process.

The key value is simple: ESGCheck helps a company move from scattered documents and uncertainty to a clearer, more structured understanding of where it stands.

## 12. Document Readiness and Guided Upload Logic

ESGCheck should not treat document collection as a one-time, all-or-nothing requirement. Most SMEs will not have a complete ESG evidence base at the beginning, and this should be treated as expected rather than as a failure.

The product should therefore include guided document-readiness logic. Instead of showing companies a long and overwhelming list of possible ESG documents, ESGCheck should help them begin with a small set of high-value materials and progressively improve the quality of their readiness overview as more evidence is added.

This supports a staged onboarding model:

- **Quick Start**: core documents such as an annual report or financial statements, electricity bills, HR headcount information, a code of conduct, and basic governance or board information.
- **Improve Readiness**: additional operational and social evidence such as gas or fuel invoices, health and safety information, training records, business travel data, or relevant internal policies.
- **Deeper Readiness**: more advanced evidence such as supplier data, risk management information, pay gap analysis, prior ESG or sustainability reports, materiality-related information, and stakeholder engagement summaries.

This staged approach turns ESG data collection from an intimidating document request into a guided readiness journey. It also gives ESGCheck a stronger product hook: companies can begin with what they have, receive a first overview, and then improve their readiness over time by adding missing evidence.

The product structure should make evidence completeness explicit:

- **ESG Readiness Overview**: what the available evidence suggests.
- **Evidence / Data Readiness Score**: how complete the uploaded evidence base is.
- **Gap List**: which documents or data are missing or insufficient.
- **Next Upload Recommendations**: what the company should add next to improve the overview.

This helps avoid false precision. Instead of implying that a limited evidence base produces a complete ESG result, ESGCheck can clearly state that the overview is based on available evidence and show what should be added next.
