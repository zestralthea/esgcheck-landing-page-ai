# ESGCheck Document Readiness Checklist

This is an internal product and methodology checklist for ESGCheck. It should inform onboarding, missing-evidence recommendations, and the Evidence / Data Readiness Score. It is not intended to be used as website copy or as a mandatory document request shown to users all at once.

The core principle is simple: most target SMEs will not have all documents at the beginning. That is expected. ESGCheck should help companies start with what they have and improve the evidence base over time.

## Product Use

Use this checklist to support:

- staged upload onboarding
- document/evidence readiness scoring
- missing-evidence detection
- next upload recommendations
- expert review and methodology refinement

## Staged Upload Model

### Quick Start

Minimum high-value evidence for a first readiness overview:

- annual report or financial statements
- electricity bills
- HR headcount report
- code of conduct
- board composition or basic governance information

### Improve Readiness

Additional evidence that improves coverage across environmental, social, and governance topics:

- gas, fuel, or heating invoices
- health and safety report or incident register
- training records
- business travel data
- relevant internal policies

### Deeper Readiness

More advanced evidence for stronger outputs and more complete reporting preparation:

- supplier data
- risk framework or enterprise risk management information
- pay gap analysis
- prior ESG or sustainability report
- materiality-related information
- stakeholder engagement summary

## Source Document Groups

### Environmental

Energy and emissions:

- electricity bills, monthly or annual, for Scope 2 emissions
- gas, fuel, and heating invoices for Scope 1 emissions
- fleet fuel consumption logs for company vehicles
- business travel reports, including flights, hotels, and rental cars, for Scope 3
- energy audit reports

Water and waste:

- water utility bills
- waste management invoices and tonnage reports
- recycling certificates or statements from waste contractors

Operations and facilities:

- lease agreements or property records to define the reporting boundary
- facilities management reports
- ISO 14001 environmental management certificates, if applicable

Supply chain and Scope 3:

- procurement spend data by supplier category
- supplier questionnaire responses
- freight and logistics data, including tonnes-km by transport mode

### Social

Workforce:

- HR headcount reports, including total employees, full-time versus part-time, gender, and country
- employee turnover or attrition reports
- recruitment data, including new hires by gender and age group
- payroll data or gender pay gap analysis
- employee satisfaction or engagement survey results

Health and safety:

- lost time injury reports or RIDDOR logs
- health and safety incident register
- occupational health reports
- health and safety audit reports or certifications, including ISO 45001 if applicable

Training and development:

- training hours log per employee
- learning and development programme descriptions
- mandatory compliance training completion records

Community and social impact:

- corporate social responsibility programme descriptions
- charitable giving and volunteering records
- community investment reports

Supply chain social evidence:

- supplier code of conduct
- modern slavery statement
- supplier audit results or self-assessment questionnaires

### Governance

Corporate structure:

- articles of association or company statutes
- organisational chart
- board composition and biographies, including gender, independence, tenure, and skills
- board and committee meeting minutes or summaries

Ethics and compliance:

- code of conduct or code of ethics
- anti-bribery and corruption policy
- whistleblower policy and report
- compliance training records
- regulatory fines or breaches, whether disclosed or internal

Risk management:

- enterprise risk management framework
- climate risk assessment, especially for TCFD-oriented work
- audit committee charter and reports
- internal audit findings

Data, privacy, and cybersecurity:

- data protection or privacy policy
- GDPR compliance documentation
- cybersecurity incident log or summary
- ISO 27001 certificate, if applicable

Remuneration:

- executive compensation policy
- CEO-to-median-worker pay ratio, if available
- remuneration committee report

### Financial and Strategic

- annual report or financial statements for the most recent year
- business strategy or sustainability strategy document
- previous ESG or sustainability reports for year-over-year comparison
- materiality assessment results, if one has been conducted
- stakeholder engagement summary

## SME Completeness Tiers

### Minimum Viable

Typical evidence:

- utility bills
- HR headcount
- basic policies

Expected output quality:

- executive summary
- partial environmental, social, and governance sections
- clear gap flags

### Good

Typical evidence:

- minimum viable evidence
- travel data
- health and safety report
- board information
- code of conduct

Expected output quality:

- around 70 percent complete coverage across sections
- stronger evidence mapping
- clearer gap and next-step recommendations

### Comprehensive

Typical evidence:

- good-tier evidence
- supplier data
- pay gap analysis
- risk framework
- prior ESG or sustainability report

Expected output quality:

- full report-oriented output
- stronger framework mapping
- possible GRI index support where methodology and evidence quality allow it

## Evidence / Data Readiness Score

The Evidence / Data Readiness Score should indicate how complete and useful the uploaded evidence base is. It should be separate from the ESG Readiness Overview.

The score should be influenced by:

- coverage across environmental, social, governance, and strategic document groups
- quality and recency of uploaded evidence
- whether the company has enough evidence for each material topic
- number and severity of missing evidence gaps
- whether next upload recommendations are available and actionable

This prevents false precision. ESGCheck can give a first overview based on available evidence while clearly showing that the evidence base is incomplete and what should be added next.
