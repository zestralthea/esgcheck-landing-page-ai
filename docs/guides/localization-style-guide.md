# ESGCheck Localization Style Guide

## Purpose
This guide keeps future `de` and `fr` copy aligned with ESGCheck's positioning: practical, credible, privacy-conscious, clear, and trustworthy.

## Tone Rules
- Write in formal B2B language: `Sie` in German, `vous` in French.
- Sound grounded and useful, not promotional or AI-hyped.
- Adapt wording for Swiss business readers without changing the product promise.
- Keep claims careful. Do not imply certification, formal assurance, legal compliance, or validated reporting quality where the English source does not.

## Locale Direction
- `de`: Swiss High German. Keep umlauts, use `ss` in place of the German sharp s, prefer `KMU`, and use Swiss-relevant business wording such as `Verwaltungsrat` where appropriate.
- `fr`: Suisse romande business French. Keep the tone measured, clear, and professional. Prefer natural Swiss-market phrasing over literal translation.

## Term Decisions
- Keep these terms in English when they read naturally in UI or product context: `Roadmap`, `Core Team`, `Technical Lead`, `CFO`, `ESG`, `GRI`, `DACH`, `MVP`, `Scope 1`, `Scope 2`.
- Localize surrounding prose freely so the sentence feels natural in-market.
- If an English startup term feels awkward in running text, prefer a natural local phrasing over a literal calque.

## Do / Don't
- Do keep wording concrete: documents, scores, gaps, next steps, privacy, trust.
- Do prefer concise business phrasing over slogan-style marketing copy.
- Do reflect Swiss market context when a more specific local term improves clarity.
- Don't add new product claims, stronger promises, or regulatory implications.
- Don't translate mechanically from English if a Swiss-market formulation is clearer.
- Don't use the German sharp s in localized copy.

## Review Check
- Compare every localized change back to the English source meaning.
- Confirm the copy still sounds practical, credible, and privacy-conscious.
- Confirm accents and umlauts are preserved in UTF-8.
